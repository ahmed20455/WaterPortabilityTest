Major Waterborne Diseases

Cholera

Cholera is an infectious disease caused by the Vibrio cholerae bacterium. It leads to severe diarrhea and dehydration, potentially causing death if untreated.

Typhoid Fever

Typhoid fever is caused by the Salmonella typhi bacterium. It spreads through contaminated food and water and can lead to high fever, abdominal pain, and in severe cases, complications affecting multiple organs.

Hepatitis A

Hepatitis A is a viral liver infection transmitted through contaminated water or food. It can cause flu-like symptoms and jaundice, with symptoms ranging from mild to severe.

Cryptosporidiosis

Cryptosporidiosis is caused by the protozoan parasite Cryptosporidium. It spreads through ingestion of contaminated water or food, or through contact with infected persons or animals. Symptoms include watery diarrhea, stomach cramps, nausea, and fever.

Amoebiasis

Amoebiasis is caused by the protozoan parasite Entamoeba histolytica. It spreads through ingestion of contaminated food or water containing cysts of the parasite. Symptoms range from mild diarrhea to dysentery (with blood and mucus in stools) and can lead to severe complications.
